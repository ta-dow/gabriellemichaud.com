Outdoor Adventures






When embarking on a camping trip, being well-prepared is key. Here's a list of essential items I always bring along:



Headlamp
To light up the night and navigate in the dark.
Camping Hammock + Stakes
For a comfortable night's sleep and securing shelter.
Sleeping Bag
To keep warm and cozy in the great outdoors.
Sleeping Pad
Adds comfort and insulation for a good night's rest.
Jetboil
A portable stove for quick cooking and water purification.
Wooden Spoon
Handy for cooking and stirring meals.
Sponge
For keeping your cookware clean.
Multipurpose Washing Detergent
Essential for cleaning up dishes and utensils.
Towel
To dry off and keep things tidy.
Toilet Paper + Hand Sanitizer
Hygiene essentials for the great outdoors.
Digging Spoon
Useful for digging cat holes for waste disposal.
Toothbrush + Toothpaste
Maintain oral hygiene, even in the wild.
Charger + Cable
Stay connected and powered up when necessary.
Rope
Handy for various campsite tasks and emergencies.
Duct Tape
A versatile fix-all for equipment and gear.
Nalgenes
Durable water bottle by day and safe hot water bottle by night
Food
trail mix + granola bars
oats
apples
bread + rice + pasta
miso soup packets
dry-cured sausages
mac and cheese ration packs
Merino Wool Base Layers
Comfortable and insulating clothing.
Down Jacket
Keep warm during chilly nights.
Goretex Jacket + Pants
Waterproof and breathable outer layers for unpredictable weather.
First Aid Kit
ibuprofen
acetaminophen
antihistamines
Vaseline lip balm
badages
burn gel
ace bandage
tweezers and scissors
saline solution
isopropyl alcohol pads
hemostatic gauze
